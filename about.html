About:

I went to the University of California, Berkeley as a MasterCard Foundation Scholar and earned my Bachelor of Arts degree in Economics (BA Econ).
The MasterCard Foundation scholarship-I earned-is a full ride scholarship equivalent to $300,000 awarded to academically high achieving high school graudates from Sub Saharan Africa who are passionate about social change and entrepreneurship, but also economically disadvantaged to afford a world class education. Before I was selected to be a MasterCard scholar at Berkeley, I had started and was running two small scale companies in my home country, Rwanda.

I also have a Certificate in Data Analysis for Pre-MBA professionals from the Imperial College London Business School. I earned this highly competitive and demanding certificate within 6 weeks, devoting about 6 hours of study per week while working on other projects.

I have great interest in the intersectionality of Economics, Finance, Data and Public Policy in driving change in Corporations, Developing Countries and Emerging Markets, and in solving the global economic and social challenges.

My goal in the next five years is to be a successful and impactful leader in international Institutions such as multinational corporations, NGOs, Consultancies, Government Agencies and the global private sector. As a leader, I also want to promoting a more integrated global environment, the climate change issue, equality, socially transformative capitalism, and work to eradicate global poverty.

I am proficient and skilled in Data Analysis and Economic Theory. I can apply economic theory, econometrics and statistics to interpret data and explain world issues through an economist lense. I am comfortable with industry dashboarding tools such as Microsoft Excel, Microsoft Power BI and Tableau. I am also proficient and knowledgeable in Statistical and Econometrics Software like Microsoft Excel, Python, R, Stata, SPSS and Matlab. Other technical skills include HTML, CSS and JavaScript which I used to build this website.
