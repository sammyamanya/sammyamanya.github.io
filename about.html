About:

I was a Mastercard Foundation Scholar at the University of California, Berkeley where I received my Bachelors Degree in Economics. The Mastercard Foundation scholarship is a full scholarship equivalent to $300,000 awarded to academically high achieving high school graudates from Sub Saharan Africa who are passionate about social change and entrepreneurship, but also economically disadvantaged to afford a world class education. Before I was selected to be a Mastercard Scholar at Berkeley, I had started and was running two small scale companies in my home country, Rwanda.

I also hold a Certificate in Data Analysis for Pre-MBA Professionals from Imperial College London Business School. I earned this highly competitive and demanding certificate within 6 weeks, by devoting 10 hours of study per week alongside other projects.

I have great interest in the intersectionality of economics, finance, data and public policy in driving positive change in Corporations, Institutions, Developing Countries and Emerging Markets, and in solving global economic and social challenges.

I am proficient and skilled in economic theory, economic technical writing, and econometrics and statistical analysis. I am skilled in industry dashboarding and data analysis tools such as MS Excel Dashboards, MS Power BI and Tableau. I am also proficient and knowledgeable in statistical and econometrics software and programing: I have intermediate skills in Python, R, Stata, SPSS, SAS, and Matlab, and advanced skills in MS Office Suite. Other technical skills include HTML, CSS, JavaScript and Github which I used to build this website.

My goal in the next five years is to be a change maker and impactful leader in local and international institutions such as multinational corporations, NGOs, consultancies, government agencies, and the global private sector. As a leader, I also want to promote a more integrated global environment, the climate change issue, equality, socially transformative capitalism, and work to eradicate global poverty.
